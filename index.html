The Problem

Every productivity app assumes you'll remember to use it.

None of them make sure you do.

⏰
Time just disappears
▼

It's 9am and you're going to start in a minute. Then it's 2pm. You didn't nap. You didn't even really do anything. The time just — went. There was no decision to waste it. It evaporated. And now the day feels broken before it even started, and the guilt of those lost hours makes the next ones even harder to use.

"I looked up and it was dark outside. I have no idea what happened to my day."

📋
Your to-do list is a graveyard
▼

You opened the app with good intentions. You added the tasks. You even color-coded them. And then life happened — and now every time you open it, it's just a list of every time you tried and didn't follow through. It stops being a tool and starts being evidence. So you stop opening it. And then you lose the app entirely. And then you download a new one and start over. Again.

"I have three different to-do apps on my phone and I haven't opened any of them in two weeks."

🧱
You can't make yourself start
▼

You're not avoiding it because you don't care. You care deeply — sometimes too deeply. But there's a wall between you and the first step, and no amount of willpower moves it. You sit down. You open the document. You close it. You make coffee. You sit back down. The task is still there. So are you. And somehow nothing happens. For hours. This is task initiation dysfunction — and it's one of the most exhausting, invisible parts of having ADHD.

"I told myself I just needed to write one sentence. I still didn't do it."

🌀
Everything feels equally urgent
▼

The ADHD brain doesn't have a reliable priority filter. The work deadline and the dishes and the text you forgot to reply to and the form you've been avoiding for three weeks — they all scream at the same volume. So instead of picking one, you pick none. You stand in the middle of your own life feeling completely frozen, not because you don't know what needs doing, but because everything needs doing and your brain refuses to rank them.

"I made a list of everything I needed to do. Then I sat and stared at it for 45 minutes."

😔
The shame is the worst part
▼

It's not just the missed tasks. It's the look on someone's face when you forgot again. It's apologizing for the same thing for the hundredth time. It's knowing you're smart enough, caring enough, capable enough — and still somehow not being able to show up the way the world expects. The shame doesn't just hurt. It makes everything harder. And most apps, without meaning to, pile more of it on every single day.

"I don't tell people I have ADHD anymore. It just sounds like an excuse for being a mess."

🔋
You're exhausted from pretending to be okay
▼

There's a version of you that most people see — the one who holds it together, laughs it off, figures it out at the last second. What they don't see is how much energy that performance takes. The constant mental load of tracking everything your brain won't hold onto. The late nights catching up. The hypervigilance. The exhaustion that doesn't go away even after a full night's sleep. ADHD isn't just hard to live with. It's hard to live with while pretending you're fine.

"I'm so tired of working twice as hard just to seem normal."

"I've downloaded every productivity app out there. I use each one for about four days before I forget it exists."

— Pretty much every adult with ADHD, ever.
